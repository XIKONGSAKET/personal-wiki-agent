# Personal Decisions Profile

For major life decisions: career moves, relocation, education, relationship transitions, and big purchases.

## When to use

- Choosing between job offers or career paths
- Deciding to relocate (city or country)
- Choosing whether / where to study further
- Major financial decisions (house, business, large investment)
- Life transitions (marriage, divorce, having children, retirement)
- Anything where the reversibility cost is high

## Phase customizations

### Phase 1 — Understanding
**Scoping questions:**
- What outcome would you regret most missing in 10 years?
- What's the reversibility — if this turns out wrong, what's the cost to undo?
- What's the time pressure for a decision?
- Who else is affected by this?
- What constraints are real vs. assumed?

### Phase 2 — Context
**Tier 3 search topics:**
- If career: industry trends, salary benchmarks, hiring climate
- If location: cost of living, visa / legal, climate, community
- If education: program outcomes data, ROI benchmarks
- If financial: tax implications, market conditions
- Always: regulatory or policy context

### Phase 3 — Generation
**Variant axes specific to personal decisions:**
- Stay vs. change
- Aggressive change vs. incremental step
- Optimizing for income vs. optimizing for time / freedom
- Optimizing for resume vs. optimizing for life satisfaction
- Solo decision vs. negotiated with stakeholders

Include at least one "do nothing" or "delay 12 months" variant.

### Phase 4 — Deep Dive
**Specification must include:**
- What this looks like 6 months in
- What this looks like 5 years in
- Who is affected and how
- Reversibility cost (in money, time, relationships)
- Optionality preserved or lost

### Phase 5 — Critique
**Premortem questions specific to personal decisions:**
- What's the most likely cause of regret in 5 years?
- What if the situation that made this attractive disappears?
- What if your personal circumstances (health, relationships, family) change?
- What if the income / lifestyle expectation is wrong?
- What if the optionality you're giving up matters more than you think?

### Phase 6 — Plan
**Planner deliverables specific to personal decisions:**
- 30-, 90-, and 365-day checkpoints
- Reversibility plan (how to undo if needed)
- Conversations to have with affected people, in sequence
- Financial buffer required
- Sunk-cost trigger: at what point do you know it's not working

## Frameworks heavily used

- Reversibility (one-way vs. two-way doors) — Bezos
- Expected Value with probability-weighted outcomes
- Regret minimization (10-year-future-self lens)
- Inversion (what would guarantee regret?)
- Second-order effects
- Time-discount asymmetry (we under-weight long-term)

## Anti-patterns specific to personal decisions

- Optimizing for the most recent emotion
- Using career / earnings as the only axis when life satisfaction differs
- Ignoring the impact on people close to you
- Treating a reversible decision as irreversible (and thus over-analyzing)
- Treating an irreversible decision as reversible (and thus under-analyzing)
- Defaulting to status quo without examining whether the status quo is what you'd choose afresh

## Critical risks to surface in critique

1. Decision is being made under acute emotional state (fear, FOMO, grief, infatuation)
2. Reversibility cost dramatically underestimated
3. Key affected person not consulted (will become a problem)
4. Financial buffer insufficient for failure scenarios
5. The decision conflates two separate decisions that should be made independently

## Special note

This profile is the one place where the coordinator should be especially careful about pushing back vs. respecting autonomy. Personal decisions are the user's to make. The tool's job is to ensure they're made with eyes open — not to argue them out of an autonomous choice.

If the user says "I've thought about all of this and I want to proceed," the coordinator should accept that and shift to Phase 6 planning support.
