# Content Strategy Profile

For creators choosing a niche, format, channel mix, and monetization strategy.

## When to use

- Starting a new content channel (YouTube, TikTok, podcast, newsletter, blog)
- Pivoting an existing channel
- Choosing between niches
- Designing a monetization stack
- Editorial / format strategy

## Phase customizations

### Phase 1 — Understanding
**Scoping questions:**
- What's the desired outcome — revenue, audience, influence, hobby?
- Time availability per week?
- Existing presence or starting from zero?
- Comfort on-camera, voiceover-only, or fully faceless?
- Topical interests and depth of expertise?
- Risk tolerance for niche bets vs. proven niches?

### Phase 2 — Context
**Tier 3 search topics:**
- Top channels in the candidate niches and their formats
- Current RPM/CPM data per niche
- Platform policy shifts (monetization rules, AI-content disclosure, algorithm changes)
- Saturation level — direct competitors at similar scope
- Recent viral patterns / format trends

### Phase 3 — Generation
**Variant axes specific to content:**
- Niche scope: narrow (specialist) vs. broad (mixed-topic)
- Format: short-form (Shorts/Reels/TikTok) vs. long-form vs. hybrid
- Production model: solo / batch / outsourced
- Persona: face-on-camera / AI avatar / fully faceless / animated
- Monetization sequence: ads-first vs. affiliate-first vs. subscription-first

Force at least one variant with low production cost.

### Phase 4 — Deep Dive
**Specification must include:**
- Channel name and handle (verify availability across YT/TikTok/IG)
- Hook formula (extracted from viral references in the niche)
- Title formula
- First 20–30 video / episode / post topics
- Production workflow with time estimate
- Cost per piece of content

### Phase 5 — Critique
**Premortem questions specific to content:**
- What's the most likely cause of channel abandonment by month 6?
- What if 30 videos in, nothing crosses 10K views?
- What if a platform algorithm change halves your reach?
- What if a copyright strike or AI-content policy issues a takedown?
- What if the niche turns out to be saturated by an established player?

### Phase 6 — Plan
**Planner deliverables specific to content:**
- Mini-MVP: 5 pieces of content as private pilot, measuring real production time
- 60- or 90-day launch plan with posting cadence
- Tracking spreadsheet template (views, retention, like rate, sub gain)
- Green-light criteria: explicit metrics, not "if it feels good"
- Burnout mitigation (batch production, content buffer)

## Frameworks heavily used

- Hook–Setup–Payoff structure
- AARRR for content (Acquisition / Activation / Retention)
- North Star Metric (subs, watch time, conversions)
- Affiliate stack design
- Sponsorship rate cards by tier
- ICE for topic prioritization

## Anti-patterns specific to content

- Posting cadence misaligned with sustainable solo capacity → burnout
- Choosing a niche based on RPM alone without consideration for personal interest
- Copying viral references without understanding what made them work
- Ignoring copyright on stock B-roll and assuming fair use applies
- Trying to monetize via ads when affiliate or product would yield 10x

## Critical risks to surface in critique

1. Production model not sustainable for 90+ days solo
2. Niche saturation by established players makes growth ceiling low
3. Topic-selection drift toward niche / hobbyist scope (kills mass appeal)
4. Platform policy change demonetizes the format (AI-content, copyright B-roll)
5. Monetization timeline (6+ months) exceeds creator's motivation runway
