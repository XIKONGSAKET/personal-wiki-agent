# Startup Profile

For founders ideating a product, business, or pre-MVP concept.

## When to use

- Validating a business idea
- Choosing between business models
- Deciding GTM strategy
- Pre-fundraising strategy work
- Pivoting an existing startup

## Phase customizations

### Phase 1 — Understanding
**Scoping questions:**
- What problem are you solving, for whom?
- What's your unfair advantage (network, expertise, capital, timing)?
- Solo founder, partnership, or team?
- Budget and runway?
- Existing traction, if any?

### Phase 2 — Context
**Tier 3 search topics:**
- Direct competitors and their fundraising history
- Market sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM) — only with credible sources
- Adjacent successful businesses (analog reasoning)
- Recent funding climate in the sector
- Relevant regulatory exposure

### Phase 3 — Generation
**Variant axes specific to startups:**
- Business model: subscription vs. transactional vs. ads vs. marketplace vs. usage-based
- Customer: B2C vs. B2B vs. prosumer vs. enterprise
- Distribution: PLG vs. sales-led vs. partnership vs. organic content
- Capital structure: bootstrapped vs. venture-backed vs. profit-first

Force at least one variant with a counter-intuitive combination.

### Phase 4 — Deep Dive
**Specification must include:**
- ICP (ideal customer profile) — specific, not "SMBs"
- Pricing model and price anchor
- Unit economics: LTV, CAC, payback period
- Initial GTM motion
- First 10 customers — how do you get them concretely

### Phase 5 — Critique
**Premortem questions specific to startups:**
- What's the most likely reason this company shuts down in 24 months?
- What if the ICP is wrong?
- What if growth doesn't reach the CAC payback threshold?
- What if a well-funded competitor enters the space?
- What if the founder's runway runs out before product-market fit?

### Phase 6 — Plan
**Planner deliverables specific to startups:**
- 90-day plan to validate core assumption
- Capital deployment plan
- Hiring sequence (if applicable)
- Customer acquisition first 10 funnel
- Green-light criteria for fundraising vs. bootstrapping

## Frameworks heavily used

- LTV / CAC ratio (target ≥ 3x)
- Payback period (target ≤ 12 months for SMB, ≤ 18 for enterprise)
- Jobs-to-be-Done
- Beachhead market selection
- AARRR (Acquisition / Activation / Retention / Referral / Revenue)
- Crossing the Chasm
- Lean Canvas

## Anti-patterns specific to startups

- Vague TAMs ("the market is huge") — demand specific source-backed numbers
- "We'll worry about monetization later" — surface the model early
- Founder-market fit hand-waving — push for specifics on unfair advantage
- Skipping pre-launch validation in favor of building
- Assuming distribution will be free or viral

## Critical risks to surface in critique

1. Founder runway shorter than time-to-PMF
2. Pricing model mismatched with customer willingness-to-pay
3. CAC ceiling makes unit economics impossible
4. Founding team gap in critical skill (sales, technical, design)
5. Distribution channel saturated or shifting (algorithm changes, paid ad cost inflation)
